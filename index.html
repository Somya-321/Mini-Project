Cold Working

Cold working is a metal forming process performed below the recrystallization temperature, typically at room temperature. It increases strength and hardness by introducing plastic deformation and raising dislocation density. Common methods include forging, extrusion, rolling, drawing, and sheet metal forming, used for thin sheets and precision parts. The process enhances surface finish, dimensional accuracy, and fatigue resistance. However, excessive deformation can cause strain hardening and reduce ductility. Heat treatments like annealing are often applied to restore ductility for further processing.
