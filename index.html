Cold Working

Cold working is a process that shapes metal at room temperature or slightly above, below its recrystallization temperature. It strengthens and hardens the material by increasing dislocation density in its crystal structure. Common cold working methods include rolling, drawing, bending, and stamping, often used for thin sheets, wires, and structural components. Benefits include improved strength, surface finish, and dimensional accuracy, but excessive cold working can cause strain hardening, which may require heat treatment to restore ductility.
